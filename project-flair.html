About FLAIR

The Flying Laboratory for Airborne Isotope Research (FLAIR) project is a multi-disciplinary effort to design, build, and fly an atmospheric-sampling aircraft capable of measuring and transmitting water stable isotope data for environmental research. This work is conducted as part of the ASEN 5018 Graduate Projects program at the University of Colorado (CU) Boulder, enabling students to gain full-length engineering experience while delivering a functional scientific payload.

The aircraft's experimental system integrates a Vaisala HMP60 humidity and temperature sensor, a multi-pass Herriott cell laser optical cavity, and a customized airflow channel mounted within the payload cavity of a Black Swift S3 UAV. During flight, ambient air is actively pulled through the front-end inlet, conditioned through a flow straightener, and directed through the Herriott cell, where multiple internal reflections effectively increase the optical path length — enhancing absorption sensitivity and allowing for more precise δ¹⁸O detection.
