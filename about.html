Who I Am

About Me

Aerospace Engineering · Structures, Materials & Fluids · Boulder, Colorado · Open to relocate

I am a graduate student in aerospace engineering at the University of Colorado Boulder, specializing in structures, fluids, and materials. My study focuses on the intersection of simulation, design, and fabrication. I am passionate about addressing complex engineering challenges and effectively guiding projects from initial CAD sketches through to validated simulations and the development of physical prototypes.

My technical work primarily involves finite element analysis (FEA) and computational fluid dynamics (CFD). I utilize ABAQUS and ANSYS for structural analyses, including stress, deformation, and failure modes, while employing ANSYS Fluent, CFX, and OpenFOAM for modeling aerodynamic flows and multiphysics problems. I develop simulations from the ground up, carefully considering meshing strategies, boundary conditions, solver selection, and result interpretation. Additionally, I design using SolidWorks, SolidEdge, NX, Onshape, and AutoCAD, recognizing the significant impact of upstream design decisions on the accuracy of downstream simulation results.

Research Interests
Structural Analysis & FEA Stress, deformation, and failure mode prediction in structures using ABAQUS and ANSYS. Optimization of analysis by different meshing and time integrations and also designs.
Computational Fluid Dynamics Aerodynamic flow modeling and multiphysics problems built from scratch in ANSYS Fluent, CFX, and OpenFOAM with careful attention to meshing strategy, solver selection, and result interpretation.
Design for Manufacture Bridging the gap between simulation and physical fabrication by applying DFM principles for 3D printing and prototyping, where most design decisions directly shape what is easily manufacturable and how well it performs.
Multiphysics Simulation Coupled structural & thermal and fluid & structure interaction analyses where aerodynamic pressure loads feed directly into structural deformation models.
VTOL & UAV Systems Payload integration and airframe structural design for large VTOL drones, with hands-on experience through the FLAIR graduate project at CU Boulder.
Composite Materials CNT-reinforced magnesium matrix composites fabricated via friction stir processing and stir casting which can be characterized through SEM, XRF, and mechanical testing, which lead to a Springer publication in 2024.
